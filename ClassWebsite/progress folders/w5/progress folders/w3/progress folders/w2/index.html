Biography

Hi there! My name is Felicia. Nice to meet ya! Here's a little bit about myself.

Early Life

Ever since I can remember, I have loved art. When I was little, my favorite pass time was drawing. I would always draw and to this day we have boxes full of drawings my mother could never throw away. Art and artistry has always been a part of my life. Throughout my childhood and late adolescence, I completed about six hours of figure skating training and 9 hours of synchronized swimming training every week. I became a perfectionnist and a high achiever, thanks to this. Everything I do has to be near perfect, otherwise it doesn't meet my standards. In competition, I would accept nothing but a gold medal so if I didn't get it, I worked even more for the next comeptition. This principle applied for almost every other aspect of my life so far.

2014 - 2016

Towards the end of highschool, I got quite tired of the sports and the constant discipline, and wanted to try something new. I, however lost track of what I wanted and became unsure about many things. So, the many hours spent on sports up until now ended up empty. I became confused, I was not accepted into the programs I applied to in CEGEP and did not know what to do. For one year, I attended night school to get some math and science classes I did not get in highschool to get into the program I originally applied to which was Computer Science. At the end of that year, I changed my mind and went into Social Science as a day student and spent a year trying to find what career I would like to go for. I wasn't really able to find anything. I did not have a portfolio for art programs and wasn't really interested in sciences. I decided that the most promising career was still Computer Science as the field is very broad and ever expanding. Thus began my career as a program developer.

2016 - 2019

The following three years were spent getting a technical CEGEP diploma as a developer with the aim of going to McGill University in Software Engineering. Through the highs and lows of my program, I saw through the end. The discipline and hard work habits I had developped with my sports paid off and helped me get through my program without mistake. Even though I am not much of a technical matth person, I worked hard and managed to get the right R score to get me into McGill in Software Engineering. I had lost hope many times, because the math and technicality of computer science made things really difficult for me, but my last stage made me see how much I really enjoyed developing programs. So, witha heart full of confidence, I accepted McGill's offer and in 2019 began my Software Engineering program as a freshman.

2019 - Present

That year was really tough for me, more than I could've ever imagined. And I hated every minute of it. It all confused me so much again. My dream of graduating McGill in a promising career might not come true after all my hard work. I knew I really liked front end development and design, but McGill offered nothing of the sort. I was puzzled and did not know what to do. Until a very good friend of mine told me about his program: Computation Arts at Concordia University. Learning about everything he now knows and learned in this program showed me light at the end of the tunnel and filled me with hope. So, as I did not have a portfolio, with all of his help and so near the deadline, I worked day and night on putting my best work together to make a pristine portfolio. Finally, I submitted everything and thus began the long wait to get an answer back. It was the most stressful time I'd felt in a long time, because if i were not accepted I wouldn't have known what more to do. And this one day, I woke up, looked at my phone and saw an email received from Concordia. My heart skipped a beat and I forgot how to breathe. I opened the email and there it was, the answer. I was accepted! Out of 150~ applications, I was one of the 40 that were accepted. I couldn't stop crying of joy! I ran upstairs to let my family know, and it was the most relief I've felt in my life. I don't think I've ever felt this happy in my life until this point.

From this point on, my new goal is to become an excellent UX Designer and Developer. I want to perfect my skills in design and in front end development to be able to contribute to beautiful websites that are fun and pleasant to use. And along the way, I hope to learn many things that can deepend my understanding in subjects that I love otherwise.
