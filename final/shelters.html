Shelters Aren't the Best...

It is easy to believe that shelters are the answer; that if you no longer need a pet you can just leave them at the nearest shelter where they will take care of the animal until someone else will just adopt them. Realistically, it is unfortunately not that way. Many shelters require financial and human resources to function, they cannot afford to have a large number of animals. When there are too many animals in a shelter, most often, they begin to euthanise the animals with decreased chances of adoption; those who've been there longest, the elder animals, those with disabilities, etc. So it is possible that your pet may simply live out the rest of its days in a cold cage, with minimum human contact until it is killed.

Of course, unforseen situation that force people to give up their pets are entirely possible. In the case that a family must do so, it is best to sit down and really do research on which orgnizations work hard to rescue animals from death, shelters that will not kill their animals and that care to make each animal feel loved until they find a forever home. Looking for a new adoptive family is also possible, as long as proper research was done and the family is trustworthy and 100% willing to take the animal in.

Before adopting or gifting an animal, it is extremely important to evaluate the situation of the person receiving or adopting the animal. Animals require love, care, time and money. They will need to be cleaned after, they will need to be fed, they will need to be vaccinated, their nails will need to be trimmed dogs will need their coats will need to be brushed, they will need to be trained, dogs will need to be walked and cats' litters will need to be cleaned. They will want to play and they will want to be cuddled. But above all, they will love you like their lives depend on it because they do. Going from a warm house with a family to a cold shelter with nobody is shocking and they won't ever even be able to understand why.

So please make sure that you meet the requirements if you are adopting an animal or that the person you want to gift an animal to is ready and willing to be taking care of a little cat or a dog for the following several years.
